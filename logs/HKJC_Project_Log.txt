* **Phase 1: Relational Vault & Baseline Metrics** *(Completed prior to April 19, 2026)*

* **Phase 3: The XGBoost Oracle** *(Completed prior to April 19, 2026)*
    * Trained a Gradient Boosted Ranker using the highly stable `rank:pairwise` objective function.
    * Implemented aggressive column subsampling (`colsample_bytree=0.5`) to break Elo feature dominance and force the algorithm to map contextual physical features.
* **Phase 4: Financial Execution & Backtesting** *(Completed prior to April 19, 2026)*
    * Built a chronological Walk-Forward backtesting engine.
    * Discovered the "Softmax Longshot Bias" (inflated EV on bad horses) and pivoted to a Pure Oracle Flat-Bet strategy to isolate algorithmic signal from betting noise.
* **Phase 5: Alpha Generation (Human & Geometric Elements)** *(Completed prior to April 19, 2026)*
    * Injected Jockey Rolling Win %, Trainer Momentum, and Barrier Draw geometry into the V2.0 Matrix.
    * **Result:** The model successfully recognized that human momentum (29%) is mathematically equal to raw horse ability (29%). The AI's raw win rate jumped from 12.6% to 18.9%.
* **Phase 6 & 7: Divergence Hunting & Market Calibration** *(Completed by April 19, 2026)*
    * Sliced the AI's #1 picks against the Public Implied Odds to hunt for "Market Divergence".
    * **Result:** Proved the Efficient Market Hypothesis. The AI is incredibly accurate (43% win rate on consensus favorites), but the public betting syndicate crushes the odds, resulting in negative Expected Value. When the AI diverges from the public, its win rate collapses to 5%.

## === Architecture Audit & Unexplored Alpha (The Blind Spots) ===
*(Logged: April 20, 2026 - 19:04 PM EDT)*

During the execution of Phase 20, a comprehensive audit of the decision-tree was conducted. If the Quinella pool proves unprofitable due to track takeout, the V7 Environment Model retains massive predictive power that was misallocated. The following eight architectural "Blind Spots" represent the immediate roadmap for future Alpha generation (V11-V15):

**1. The Kelly EV Threshold (Win Pool):**
* *The Flaw:* Executing bets whenever Expected Value > 0.00% subjected the bankroll to massive volume on razor-thin margins, bleeding capital to the track rake.
* *The Pivot:* Implement a strict Edge Cutoff (`if EV > 0.05`). Starve the model of marginal bets.

**2. Monetizing the Place/Show Pools:**
* *The Flaw:* We forced highly accurate Dark Horse predictions into high-variance Quinella requirements.
* *The Pivot:* Transition Dark Horse targets into the Place/Show pools for safe, low-variance compounding when pace collapses occur.

**3. Synergy Momentum (Human Interaction Vectors):**
* *The Flaw:* Treated Jockeys and Trainers as static win-percentages.
* *The Pivot:* Engineer "Human Synergy" vectors to track momentum shifts when elite jockeys drop standard mounts to ride for specific trainers (capturing the syndicate whisper network).

**4. Natural Language Processing (The Traffic Trouble Pivot):**
* *The Flaw:* Numerical data mathematically punishes horses for bad finishes caused by traffic trouble. 
* *The Pivot (V11):* Deploy a Regex/NLP parser over HKJC Steward's Reports to flag "held up for clear running" or "checked." Engineer a `trouble_severity_score` so the algorithm can forgive mathematically poor runs.

**5. Margin-Adjusted Elo (The "Distance Beaten" Flaw):**
* *The Flaw:* Standard Pairwise Elo treats a nose victory identically to a 6-length blowout.
* *The Pivot (V12):* Scale the Elo K-factor by the `lengths_behind` column to accurately map kinetic superiority.

**6. Multi-Dimensional Surface Vectors (The Specialist Flaw):**
* *The Flaw:* Averaging a horse's ability across disparate tracks and distances into a single `pre_race_elo` score.
* *The Pivot (V13):* Split Elo into Orthogonal Vectors (`Turf_Elo`, `Dirt_Elo`, `Sprint_Elo`, `Route_Elo`) selected dynamically at prediction time.

**7. Profit-Aware Custom Loss Functions (The NDCG Trap):**
* *The Flaw:* Training XGBoost with `ndcg` optimizes for accuracy, ignoring financial odds. The AI learns to predict heavy favorites to minimize error.
* *The Pivot (V14):* Write a Custom Objective Function for Gradient Descent that injects live Pari-Mutuel odds into the training loop, forcing the AI to optimize for profit rather than positional accuracy.

**8. Multi-Way Probability Mathematics (The Pairwise Independence Flaw):**
* *The Flaw:* Pairwise math and the Harville formula fail to account for Pace Correlation and sequential dependency in a multi-agent race.
* *The Pivot (V15):* Transition to the Plackett-Luce Model (Rank-Ordered Logit) or Microsoft TrueSkill to treat the race as a sequential extraction of probabilities, solving the Harville mispricing trap.

      54, et al.). It is no longer out-of-sample. Reported ROI contains an
      unknown but potentially large overfit component.

  (C) SETTLEMENT MATH BUG — v31 credits winning bets at base * mean(6 synthetic
      odds) when only 1 of 6 combos can win. Correct settlement is the winning
      ticket's own price. Silent ROI inflator.

v32 is designed to resolve all three.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
v32 ARCHITECTURE — WALK-FORWARD ROLLING BACKTEST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Core protocol:
    For each test season t in [2016/17, ..., 2025/26]:
        Train window = seasons [t-5, t-1]   (trailing 5 seasons)
        Test window  = season t
        - Rebuild ALL stateful features (Elo, Glicko-2, PageRank) chronologically
          from scratch WITHIN the train window. Stateful features DO NOT carry
          state across the rolling boundary, to prevent future-season information
          from leaking backward through rating chains.
        - Fit XGBRanker + place/win calibrators on train window only.
        - Execute desk on test window using REAL dividends.
        - Report per-season ROI, Sharpe, max drawdown, strike rate, N bets,
          N races, avg odds taken, bankroll curve.

Aggregate reporting:
    - Per-season table (all metrics above).
    - 10-season pooled ROI with bootstrap 95% CI (1,000 resamples).
    - Per-season drawdown distribution.

Pool selection for v32:
    - Trio (matches v31 for comparability).
    - All other pools (Win, Place, Quinella, Quinella Place, Tierce, First 4,
      Quartet) scraped in parallel for v33+ pool-selection studies. Not bet
      in v32.

Settlement math (correction of Finding C):
    Actual payout = base_per_combo * O_winning_combo
    NOT          = base_per_combo * mean(O_all_combos)

SEALED HOLDOUT COMMITMENT — METHODOLOGICAL CONTRACT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The 2025/26 HKJC season is hereby SEALED.

Data partition (three tiers, NOT two):
    Tier 1 — WARM-UP:           2011/12 → 2015/16  (Elo/Glicko/PageRank seeding)
    Tier 2 — DEVELOPMENT:       2016/17 → 2024/25  (9 seasons walk-forward)
    Tier 3 — SEALED HOLDOUT:    2025/26            (1 season, untouched)

Rules of the seal — these are non-negotiable:

  1. ALL v32 development decisions are made using Tier 2 only. This includes:
     - Feature engineering choices
     - XGBRanker hyperparameters
     - Calibrator design
     - Pool selection
     - Anchor rules, leg rules, EV thresholds
     - Kelly fraction and bet sizing logic
     - Liquidity caps
     - Any structural architectural decision

  2. The sealed Tier 3 season is run EXACTLY ONCE, after v32 is fully frozen.
     The result is reported regardless of outcome. The number that comes out
     is the only honest estimate of true out-of-sample performance.

  3. If the sealed result is unsatisfactory, the response is NOT to re-tune.
     Re-tuning against the sealed result breaks the seal permanently and
     mathematically reduces v32 to having no holdout at all. The only
     legitimate responses to a bad sealed result are:
        (a) Accept it as the strategy's true expected performance.
        (b) Declare v33, develop against ALL of 2016/17-2025/26 (now fully
            development data), and seal a NEW future window (e.g., wait for
            2026/27 to complete and seal that).

  4. WHY THE WALK-FORWARD ALONE IS INSUFFICIENT:
     Walk-forward guarantees TEMPORAL ISOLATION OF THE MODEL — the XGBRanker
     for season t has provably never seen season t's data. It does NOT
     guarantee TEMPORAL ISOLATION OF THE RESEARCHER. Every time the human
     looks at a backtest result and changes the strategy in response, that
     test data has been used as training data for the human-in-the-loop
     optimizer. The sealed season is the only data where the strategy
     designer was blind.

     Formally, the deployed system is:
        Strategy = f(model_params, theta_Kelly, theta_EV, theta_anchor,
                     theta_odds_bands, feature_set, pool_choice, ...)
     The model_params are fit by gradient boosting on the train window.
     ALL OTHER theta values are fit by the human against backtest feedback.
     Without a sealed holdout, the human-fit theta values overfit invisibly.

SCHEMA DESIGN RATIONALE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - date_iso (YYYY-MM-DD) alongside date (DD/MM/YYYY) — SQLite cannot sort
    DD/MM/YYYY correctly. ISO format enables fast range queries for
    walk-forward train windows: WHERE date_iso BETWEEN ...

    all three tables. Replaces brittle (date, race_name) joins.

  - horse_id parsed from "NAME (CODE)" — CODE is HKJC's permanent ID,
    stable across name changes. Names alone are unreliable.

  - Compound indexes (jockey, date_iso) and (trainer, date_iso) —
    v32 will compute rolling jockey/trainer win rates with date-bounded
    queries. These indexes turn full-scan O(N) into O(log N).

  - Drop-and-recreate every ingest — no migration complexity, fast rebuild
    (~minutes), guarantees deterministic state. The raw CSVs are the
    source of truth.

LIMITATIONS DISCOVERED (CRITICAL — drove v3.0 re-scrape decision)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — PLACE and QUINELLA PLACE pools captured only 33% of dividend rows.
  Root cause: HKJC dividend tables use HTML rowspan attribute for multi-row
  pools. PLACE shows 3 dividend rows per race (one per placing horse), but
  only the first <tr> contains a <td>PLACE</td> cell — subsequent rows have
  rowspan="3" on the first cell, meaning continuation rows have NO pool
  cell at all (they jump straight to horse_no, dividend).

  The v2.1 scraper's match_pool() returned None on empty first cells,
  silently skipping continuation rows. Same root cause affects QUINELLA
  PLACE (3 winning pair combinations from top-3 finishers).

  Quantitative evidence:
    Pool             Actual    Expected
    WIN              14,007    ~11,300 (some multi-winner pools)
    PLACE            11,289    ~33,800 (3 per race) — 33% coverage
    QUINELLA         11,289    ~11,300
    QUINELLA PLACE   11,215    ~33,800 (3 per race) — 33% coverage
    TIERCE           11,289    ~11,300
    TRIO             16,969    ~11,300 (some dead-heats)
    FIRST 4          11,270    ~11,300
    QUARTET           8,970    not offered on all races (correct)

L2 — Distance NULL for 219 race_metadata rows (1.9%).
  All 219 are within the non-bettable population (overseas simulcasts,
  Conghua, abandoned). Zero impact on the bettable dataset (verified).
  Distance regex tightened in v3.0 to handle "1,200M", "1200m", etc.

L3 — REFUND row information loss.
  v2.1 dividend-value regex (\d{1,3}...) didn't match "REFUND" strings,
  so refund cells were silently dropped at scrape time. Cannot distinguish
  "abandoned race" from "scrape failure" at the database level. Fixed in
  v3.0 — REFUND now preserved with dividend=NULL, is_refund=1 flag.

KEY LEARNINGS RECORDED FOR FUTURE SESSIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

KL1 — TEST SCRAPES MUST INCLUDE QUANTITATIVE RATIO CHECKS
  The v2.1 scraper's L1 bug went undetected through 30 hours of full-
  scale scraping because the absolute pool counts looked "reasonable in
  isolation." Only the ratio check (PLACE rows per race should be ~3.0)
  revealed the systematic 33% coverage gap. ALL future scraper tests
  must include per-pool ratio checks against expected per-race counts.

KL2 — NETWORK ERROR HANDLING MUST DISTINGUISH TRANSIENT FROM PERMANENT
  The v3.2 scraper treated all errors equivalently — marked the date
  as processed and moved on. This is correct for permanent failures
  (overseas pages, missing meetings) but catastrophic for transient
  network issues. The gap-fill scraper fixed this by checking the
  error string for 'NAME_NOT_RESOLVED' or 'timed out' and refusing to
  mark such dates as processed. The main v3.2 scraper SHOULD be patched
  similarly if ever re-run.

KL3 — DNS FAILURES ARE THE MOST LIKELY MULTI-HOUR OUTAGE CAUSE
  Residential internet has occasional sub-hour drops. Long scrapes
  spanning overnight WILL encounter these. The gap-fill approach
  (diff against a baseline, identify missing dates, retry only those)
  is the correct general pattern for any long-running data acquisition.

KL4 — RACING INCIDENT REPORTS STARTED 04 OCTOBER 2023
  HKJC's "Racing Incident Report" table did not exist before this date.
  Any FUTURE-1 work (LLM-based trouble extraction) is limited to
  data from 2023/24 season onward — approximately 2.5 seasons available.
  This is enough for features on the sealed holdout (2025/26) and the
  final development season (2024/25) but insufficient for a walk-forward
  5-year train window that includes incident features. FUTURE-1 may
  need to be a feature only present for recent races, with the model
  handling its absence gracefully.

KL5 — HKJC PUBLISHES NON-BETTABLE TRAINING/EXHIBITION RACES
  Conghua training meetings (CH venue) appear in result archives but
  have no dividend tables. The ingest pipeline's is_bettable=0 logic
  handles these correctly: race has results + metadata + zero
  dividends → mark non-bettable. Current count: 7 non-bettable races
  across 15 seasons (~0.06%).

DECISION: REBUILD STRATEGY = CANDIDATE 1 (FULL REBUILD FROM 2011)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
For each test season t, stateful features are rebuilt by replaying the
ENTIRE race history from 2011-09-11 forward, taking pre-race snapshots,
and slicing to the train window [t-5, t-1] for model fitting.

Why Candidate 1 (vs window-start-with-defaults or hybrid):
  1. Maximum rating fidelity — established horses carry their true
     accumulated ratings into the train window, not a reset-to-1500
     fiction.
  2. Leakage properties identical to alternatives — the snapshot-then-
     advance protocol guarantees no future race influences a past
     feature, regardless of where the rebuild starts. Full rebuild is
     just an efficient snapshot computation, not a leak.
  3. Reproducibility — one canonical rating per race, deterministic,
     independent of which test season is being run.

Compute: ~4-6 min/season rebuild; ~45-60 min for full 10-season
walk-forward. Well within budget.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STATEFULFEATUREENGINE — IMPLEMENTATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
File: data_pipeline/stateful_feature_engine.py

Core abstraction — the LEAKAGE PROTOCOL:
  eng.reset()                      clear all running state
  snap = eng.snapshot_for(rid)     pre-race features (PRIOR state only)
  eng.advance_race(rid)            fold this race's result into state

Calling snapshot BEFORE advance, race-by-race in chronological order,
structurally guarantees no future race influences a past feature value.
This replaces v31's pandas .shift(1) with a structural guarantee.

Performance optimization: PageRank computed lazily (pr_dirty flag) —
recomputed only at snapshot time when the graph changed, not on every
advance. ~10x faster than naive per-race recomputation.

Determinism: no randomness in the engine. Races processed in
(date_iso, race_no) order. Identical inputs -> identical outputs.

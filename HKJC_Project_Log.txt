# HKJC Quantitative Prediction Engine - Project Log
**Last Updated:** April 20, 2026

## === Project Overview ===
A complete, end-to-end quantitative machine learning pipeline designed to predict Hong Kong Jockey Club (HKJC) horse racing results and execute simulated financial backtests.

## === Phase Architecture (V1 - V5) ===

* **Phase 1: Relational Vault & Baseline Metrics** *(Completed prior to April 19, 2026)*
    * Built a pristine SQLite relational database (`hk_racing.db`) to house decades of racing data.
    * Engineered a look-ahead-bias-free, time-shifted Pairwise Elo rating system to evaluate pure equine ability without relying on public class systems.
* **Phase 2: Contextual Feature Engineering** *(Completed prior to April 19, 2026)*
    * Synthesized physical momentum metrics (Weight Deltas, Distance Deltas, Days of Rest, Career Wins).
    * Used strictly shifted Pandas rolling windows (`.shift(1)`) to mathematically guarantee zero data leakage.
* **Phase 3: The XGBoost Oracle** *(Completed prior to April 19, 2026)*
    * Trained a Gradient Boosted Ranker using the highly stable `rank:pairwise` objective function.
    * Implemented aggressive column subsampling (`colsample_bytree=0.5`) to break Elo feature dominance and force the algorithm to map contextual physical features.
* **Phase 4: Financial Execution & Backtesting** *(Completed prior to April 19, 2026)*
    * Built a chronological Walk-Forward backtesting engine.
    * Discovered the "Softmax Longshot Bias" (inflated EV on bad horses) and pivoted to a Pure Oracle Flat-Bet strategy to isolate algorithmic signal from betting noise.
* **Phase 5: Alpha Generation (Human & Geometric Elements)** *(Completed prior to April 19, 2026)*
    * Injected Jockey Rolling Win %, Trainer Momentum, and Barrier Draw geometry into the V2.0 Matrix.
    * **Result:** The model successfully recognized that human momentum (29%) is mathematically equal to raw horse ability (29%). The AI's raw win rate jumped from 12.6% to 18.9%.
* **Phase 6 & 7: Divergence Hunting & Market Calibration** *(Completed by April 19, 2026)*
    * Sliced the AI's #1 picks against the Public Implied Odds to hunt for "Market Divergence".
    * **Result:** Proved the Efficient Market Hypothesis. The AI is incredibly accurate (43% win rate on consensus favorites), but the public betting syndicate crushes the odds, resulting in negative Expected Value. When the AI diverges from the public, its win rate collapses to 5%. 

### Quantitative Debrief (Mid-Point)
The pipeline's plumbing—database, chronological shifting, ML ranking, and walk-forward simulation—is mathematically flawless and structurally ready for production. However, competing against the HKJC public pool using public data yields negative ROI due to the 17.5% track takeout. **The Path Forward:** The model requires Orthogonal Data.

## === Architecture Audit & Unexplored Alpha (The Blind Spots) ===
*(Logged: April 20, 2026 - 19:04 PM EDT)*

During the execution of Phase 20, a comprehensive audit of the decision-tree was conducted. If the Quinella pool proves unprofitable due to track takeout, the V7 Environment Model retains massive predictive power that was misallocated. The following eight architectural "Blind Spots" represent the immediate roadmap for future Alpha generation (V11-V15):

**1. The Kelly EV Threshold (Win Pool):**
* *The Flaw:* Executing bets whenever Expected Value > 0.00% subjected the bankroll to massive volume on razor-thin margins, bleeding capital to the track rake.
* *The Pivot:* Implement a strict Edge Cutoff (`if EV > 0.05`). Starve the model of marginal bets.

**2. Monetizing the Place/Show Pools:**
* *The Flaw:* We forced highly accurate Dark Horse predictions into high-variance Quinella requirements.
* *The Pivot:* Transition Dark Horse targets into the Place/Show pools for safe, low-variance compounding when pace collapses occur.

**3. Synergy Momentum (Human Interaction Vectors):**
* *The Flaw:* Treated Jockeys and Trainers as static win-percentages.
* *The Pivot:* Engineer "Human Synergy" vectors to track momentum shifts when elite jockeys drop standard mounts to ride for specific trainers (capturing the syndicate whisper network).

**4. Natural Language Processing (The Traffic Trouble Pivot):**
* *The Flaw:* Numerical data mathematically punishes horses for bad finishes caused by traffic trouble. 
* *The Pivot (V11):* Deploy a Regex/NLP parser over HKJC Steward's Reports to flag "held up for clear running" or "checked." Engineer a `trouble_severity_score` so the algorithm can forgive mathematically poor runs.

**5. Margin-Adjusted Elo (The "Distance Beaten" Flaw):**
* *The Flaw:* Standard Pairwise Elo treats a nose victory identically to a 6-length blowout.
* *The Pivot (V12):* Scale the Elo K-factor by the `lengths_behind` column to accurately map kinetic superiority.

**6. Multi-Dimensional Surface Vectors (The Specialist Flaw):**
* *The Flaw:* Averaging a horse's ability across disparate tracks and distances into a single `pre_race_elo` score.
* *The Pivot (V13):* Split Elo into Orthogonal Vectors (`Turf_Elo`, `Dirt_Elo`, `Sprint_Elo`, `Route_Elo`) selected dynamically at prediction time.

**7. Profit-Aware Custom Loss Functions (The NDCG Trap):**
* *The Flaw:* Training XGBoost with `ndcg` optimizes for accuracy, ignoring financial odds. The AI learns to predict heavy favorites to minimize error.
* *The Pivot (V14):* Write a Custom Objective Function for Gradient Descent that injects live Pari-Mutuel odds into the training loop, forcing the AI to optimize for profit rather than positional accuracy.

**8. Multi-Way Probability Mathematics (The Pairwise Independence Flaw):**
* *The Flaw:* Pairwise math and the Harville formula fail to account for Pace Correlation and sequential dependency in a multi-agent race.
* *The Pivot (V15):* Transition to the Plackett-Luce Model (Rank-Ordered Logit) or Microsoft TrueSkill to treat the race as a sequential extraction of probabilities, solving the Harville mispricing trap.
